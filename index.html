Hello and welcome to the Pig Game

Pig game is a simple dice game first described by John Scarne in 1945. Players take turns to roll a single dice as many times as they wish, adding all roll results to a current score, but losing their gained current score for the turn if they roll a 1.

  • If the player rolls a 1, they score nothing and it becomes the next player's turn.
  • If the player rolls any other number, it is added to their current score and the player's turn continues.
  • If a player chooses to "hold", their current score is added to their total score, and it becomes the next player's turn.

The first player to score 100 or more score wins.

Big Pig

This mode adds more options to the game.

  • If the player rolls a special side of the dice can gain current score +10, +20, +30, or may lose from score -10, -20, -30.
  • If the player rolls swap. If he wants to he can swap total scores with his opponent.
  • A player can lose the game if his score is below -50.

The first player to score 200 or more score wins.

Hyper Pig

This mode adds same options as Big Pig mode. Only the difference is the player with higher score wins after timer is off. So whoever has the bigger score after 2 minutes wins!
